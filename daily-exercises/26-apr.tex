Show that $SPACE(n) \neq SPACE(n^2)$.

\textbf{Answer:}

This follows directly from the Space Hierarchy Theorem.

Take

\[
f(n) = n, \qquad g(n) = n^2.
\]

Both of these are space-constructible, and

\[
f(n) = o(g(n)).
\]

So by the Space Hierarchy Theorem,

\[
SPACE(f(n)) \subsetneq SPACE(g(n)).
\]

Substituting $f(n) = n$ and $g(n) = n^2$, we get

\[
SPACE(n) \subsetneq SPACE(n^2).
\]

In particular,

\[
SPACE(n) \neq SPACE(n^2).
\]

Hence proved.
